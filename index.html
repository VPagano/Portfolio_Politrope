Polìtropós agg. [from greek πολύτροπος,i.e. πολυ- «many-» and -τροπος, from the verb τρέπω «turn»]

Adjective associated with the Homeric figure of Odysseus literally means a person of versatile and multifaceted intellect.

In modern terms, one can call oneself a polytropós/é/óv person when he/she looks at problems from different perspectives and has a multidisciplinary approach.
